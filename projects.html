  • Aerodynamic Wing Design using Sequential Local Response Surfaces(B.Tech Project)

    This investigates the optimization strategy of Sequential Local Approximations(referred to as Multipoint Approximations by Guinta et al) coupled with Response Surface Methodology. The problem chosen for the purpose is to design the wing cross-section(defined by 7 parameters) for maximum L/D at cruise condition. The aircraft data belongs to a typical jet fighter, and the planform parameters are those of the Onera M-6 wing. Second order polynomial response surfaces in 7 variable design space, boxed by move limits, are used for the local approximation to optimize lift to drag ratio of the wing. Results generated using sequential local approximations indicate that it is an effective method for finding the local optimum in a design space.

    July 2002 – May 2003 under Prof. P.M.Mujumdar (mujumdar@aero.iitb.ac.in) and Prof. K.Sudhakar (sudhakar@aero.iitb.ac.in), Department of Aerospace Engineering , IIT Bombay
